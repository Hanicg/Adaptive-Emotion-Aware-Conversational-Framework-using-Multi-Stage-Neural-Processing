Despite remarkable progress in conversational artificial intelligence, most dialogue systems still struggle to understand one critical aspect of human communication emotion. As a result, interactions often feel mechanical and lack the empathy required in sensitive domains such as mental health support, education, and customer service. Addressing this gap, this paper introduces an Adaptive Emotion-Aware Conversational Framework that enhances machine interactions by integrating emotional intelligence into the core of dialogue generation.
The proposed system employs a dual-pipeline architecture that combines a DistilBERT-based emotion recognition module with a BlenderBot-based response generation module. A novel context injection mechanism is introduced, where the detected emotional state is embedded into the input prompt, enabling the system to dynamically adapt its tone, style, and response strategy. This approach allows the chatbot to move beyond generic replies and deliver contextually appropriate, empathetic responses.
To ensure efficiency and real-time applicability, the framework leverages optimized transformer architectures along with advanced decoding strategies, including beam search, n-gram repetition constraints, and early stopping. The system is evaluated using both quantitative metrics, such as accuracy and F1-score for emotion classification, and qualitative human evaluation focusing on fluency, relevance, and emotional alignment.
Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed framework significantly outperforms traditional non-emotion-aware conversational models, achieving improved emotional responsiveness and higher user satisfaction while maintaining computational efficiency. The modular design further enables easy extension to multimodal inputs, such as speech and facial expressions, paving the way for more comprehensive emotion-aware systems.
By bridging the gap between semantic understanding and emotional intelligence, this work contributes toward the development of next-generation human-centric conversational agents that are more natural, adaptive, and engaging.
